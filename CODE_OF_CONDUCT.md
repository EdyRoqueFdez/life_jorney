# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, the members, contributors, and leaders of the life_jorney community, pledge to make participation in our project and community a welcoming, respectful, and safe experience for everyone.

life_jorney exists for a single purpose: to improve access to unified medical history for people regardless of their economic situation, geographic location, or social standing. This humanitarian mission shapes everything we do, including how we treat one another. The people whose lives this software may affect — patients managing chronic conditions, caregivers navigating fragmented health systems, clinicians working in under-resourced settings — deserve a community built on dignity and trust.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, inclusive, and healthy community, and we welcome contributors regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We also recognize that this project brings together people from very different professional worlds: software engineers, data architects, physicians, nurses, allied health professionals, designers, translators, patient advocates, and many others. We pledge to treat each discipline with equal respect. Clinical knowledge and engineering knowledge are both essential here. Neither takes precedence over the other as a basis for human dignity within this community.

We commit to building a community where patients and people with lived experience of illness feel safe to participate, where their perspectives are valued, and where discussions about medical conditions and health scenarios are handled with the sensitivity those topics deserve.

## Our Standards

### Behavior that contributes to a positive environment

- Using welcoming and inclusive language, and defaulting to patience when communicating across professional disciplines or language barriers.
- Treating every contributor — whether a senior physician, a first-time open source contributor, or a patient sharing their experience — with equal courtesy and genuine respect.
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback graciously. Critiquing ideas, implementations, and designs without making it personal.
- Acknowledging the limits of your own expertise. Developers should approach clinical discussions with humility; clinicians should approach technical discussions with the same. Asking questions is always appropriate.
- Discussing medical conditions, diagnoses, treatment approaches, and health scenarios in general and appropriately anonymized terms. When clinical examples are needed to illustrate a feature or bug, they must be fictional or fully de-identified.
- Centering the humanitarian mission in decisions. When in doubt about a trade-off, ask: what serves the patient best?
- Accepting responsibility when we make mistakes, apologizing to those affected, and learning from the experience.
- Prioritizing what is good for the community and the people this project serves, over individual preferences or ego.

### Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form, whether public or private.
- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, deliberately inflammatory comments, personal or political attacks, or sustained disruption of discussions.
- Publishing others' private information — including physical addresses, email addresses, or any identifying details — without their explicit permission.
- Disclosing or referencing real patient data of any kind in issues, pull requests, commit messages, forum posts, chat messages, or any other project space. This includes partial identifiers, anecdotes that could identify a real person, screenshots from real clinical systems, and any data that was not explicitly constructed as synthetic or fictional. This rule is absolute. Violations will be treated as severe.
- Denigrating or dismissing contributors based on their professional background. Treating clinical contributors as less technically credible, or treating non-clinical contributors as irrelevant to healthcare decisions, is not acceptable.
- Using the project's name, community spaces, or humanitarian framing to promote commercial products, services, or agendas unrelated to the project's mission.
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting, whether or not it occurs in a project space.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders and project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior set out in this document and for taking fair, consistent, and proportionate corrective action in response to any behavior they determine to be in violation.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct. When a moderation decision is made, leaders will communicate the reasons for it where appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all community spaces associated with life_jorney, including but not limited to: the GitHub repository, issue tracker, pull requests, project wikis, mailing lists, chat channels, video calls, and any other forum created or endorsed by the project.

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces — for example, when posting on social media as a project representative, speaking at a conference on behalf of life_jorney, or acting as a designated contact for the project in an external community.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by emailing the project's conduct team.

> **Note:** the project domain has not yet been defined. A dedicated conduct email will be created and published here before the first public release. Until then, you can raise concerns through a private message to a project maintainer via GitHub.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with strict confidentiality. The identity of the reporter will not be disclosed to the subject of the report or to anyone outside the conduct team without the explicit consent of the reporter. We understand that coming forward — particularly for people who are patients or who are otherwise in vulnerable situations — requires trust, and we take that trust seriously.

When reporting, please include as much of the following as you are comfortable sharing: your contact information, the names or usernames of the people involved, a description of the incident and its context, links or screenshots if available, and any additional information you think is relevant.

### Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines when determining consequences for violations of this Code of Conduct.

**1. Correction**

_Community impact:_ Use of language or behavior that is unprofessional, unwelcoming, or otherwise contrary to community standards.

_Consequence:_ A private, written communication from community leaders identifying the specific behavior, explaining why it was problematic, and requesting a change. A public apology may be requested where appropriate.

**2. Warning**

_Community impact:_ A single significant violation, or a pattern of behavior that, while not individually severe, creates a consistently negative environment for others.

_Consequence:_ A formal warning with explicit guidance about what future behavior is expected. The individual may be asked to avoid interaction with the people involved for a specified period, including in community spaces and through external channels such as social media. Continued violations after a warning may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

**3. Temporary Ban**

_Community impact:_ A serious violation of community standards, or continued problematic behavior after a formal warning.

_Consequence:_ A temporary suspension of all participation in community spaces for a defined period. No public or private contact with the people involved, including conduct team members, is permitted during this period except through officially designated channels. Violating the terms of a temporary ban may result in a permanent ban.

**4. Permanent Ban**

_Community impact:_ A pattern of sustained violations; behavior that causes serious harm to the community or to individuals; disclosure of real patient data; harassment of a person who disclosed a health condition or who is participating in the community in a patient capacity; or any single act that demonstrates a fundamental disregard for the dignity of the people this project exists to serve.

_Consequence:_ Permanent removal from all community spaces with no path to reinstatement.
